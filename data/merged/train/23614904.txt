Bile Acid and Sterol Metabolism with Combined HMGCoA Reductase and PCSK9 Suppression.
PCSK9 inhibition markedly augments the LDL lowering action of statins.
The combination is being evaluated for long-term effects on atherosclerotic disease outcomes.
However, effects of combined treatment on hepatic cholesterol and bile acid metabolism have not yet been reported.
To study this, PCSK9-Y119X mutant (knockout) and wild type mice were treated Â± atorvastatin for 12 wks.
Atorvastatin progressively lowered plasma LDL in each group, but no differences in liver cholesterol, cholesterol ester, or total bile acid concentrations, or in plasma total bile acid levels were seen.
In contrast, atorvastatin increased fecal total bile acids (~2-fold, P<0.01) and cholesterol concentrations (~3-fold, P<0.01) vs. controls for both PCSK9-Y119X and wild type mice.
All 14 individual bile acids resolved by LC-MS, including primary, secondary, and conjugated species, reflected similar increases.
Expression of key liver bile acid synthesis genes CYP7A1 and CYP8B1 were ~2.5-fold higher with atorvastatin in both strains, but mRNA for liver bile acid export and reuptake transporters and conjugating enzymes were not unaffected.
The data suggest that hepatocyte cholesterol and bile acid homeostasis is maintained with combined PCSK9 and HMGCoA reductase inhibition through efficient liver enzymatic conversion of LDL-derived cholesterol into bile acids and excretion of both, with undisturbed enterohepatic recycling.
